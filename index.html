What to Do During a Roadside Emergency

Being prepared for a roadside emergency can help keep you safe while waiting for towing assistance. Here are a few essential tips:

1. Move to a Safe Location

If possible, move your vehicle to the shoulder of the road or a nearby parking area. This reduces the risk of further accidents and keeps you out of the flow of traffic. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.

2. Contact Javi’s Towing And Transport Orlando

Call us at (689) 400-8188. Provide your location, a description of your vehicle, and details of the issue. Their dispatchers will send the nearest available tow truck to assist you.

3. Stay Inside Your Vehicle if Safe

If you are on a busy road or in a high-traffic area, it is often safer to remain inside your vehicle with your seatbelt fastened. Exiting your car in heavy traffic can be dangerous. If you must leave your vehicle, move to a safe area away from the roadway.

4. Be Prepared with Essential Information

When you call for emergency towing, providing accurate information helps the tow truck arrive more efficiently. Be ready to share:
Your location or nearby landmarks
The type of vehicle you are driving
The nature of the problem (flat tire, engine failure, accident, etc.)

Tips for Preventing Roadside Emergencies

While not all roadside incidents are preventable, regular vehicle maintenance and preparation can reduce the likelihood of needing emergency towing. Here are a few tips:

  • Perform regular maintenance: Schedule routine oil changes, tire rotations, and fluid checks to keep your car running smoothly.
  • Inspect your tires: Regularly check tire pressure and tread depth to avoid unexpected blowouts.
  • Check your battery: To avoid sudden failures, your car battery is tested periodically, especially before long trips.
  • Carry an emergency kit: Keep a kit in your vehicle with essentials such as jumper cables, a flashlight, first aid supplies, and water.
